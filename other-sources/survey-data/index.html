    Survey Data¶

    • NFCS (National Financial Capability Study) — U.S. survey data on financial behaviors, attitudes, knowledge, and access to financial products. Six waves (2009–2024), ~25,000 respondents per wave. Free download in .dta, .csv, and .sav formats. By the FINRA Foundation.
    • LISS Panel (Longitudinal Internet Studies for the Social Sciences) — Dutch household panel of ~7,500 individuals from a probability sample drawn by Statistics Netherlands (CBS). Annual Core Study since 2007 covering income, health, education, values, and economic behavior. Free for academic research. Managed by Centerdata (Tilburg University).
    • ECB Household Finance and Consumption Survey (HFCS) — Household-level data on income, wealth, consumption, and debt across Euro area countries. Useful for studying wealth distribution, financial behavior, and the impact of monetary policy on households.
    • ECB SAFE Survey — Survey on the Access to Finance of Enterprises. Covers SME financing needs, access to credit, and perceived barriers to funding across Europe.
    • Federal Reserve Survey of Consumer Finances (SCF) — Detailed data on U.S. households' balance sheets, pensions, income, and demographics. Triennial survey with a focus on wealth distribution. Free download.
    • University of Michigan Survey of Consumers — Monthly data on U.S. consumer sentiment, expectations, and attitudes towards economic conditions. Widely used as a leading indicator in macro-finance research.
